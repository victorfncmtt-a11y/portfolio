# SKILL: Brand Strategy & Multi-Channel Content Engine

## Objective
Ingest raw business data, target profiles, or product details, and transform them into an executable, zero-fluff strategy divided into three core procedural phases.

## Execution Matrix

### PHASE 1: BRAND GUIDELINES & POSITIONING TERRITORY
Deconstruct the target brand into a highly structured architecture:
1. Core Value Proposition: The objective utility the brand provides to its market.
2. Verbal & Visual Tone Constraints: Explicit rules for alignment (e.g., technical, minimalist, high-fidelity). 
3. Strategic Territories: Define exactly 3 distinct conceptual territories the brand can authentically claim to differentiate itself.

### PHASE 2: CONTENT DIRECTION & MEDIUM SELECTION
For each approved territory, map the distribution architecture based strictly on distribution mechanics and audience consumption friction:
- Video (Short/Long-form): For high-emotion, storytelling, or high-fidelity technical demonstrations.
- Written (Social/Articles): For authority building, algorithmic distribution, and precise articulation.
- Email: For low-friction, high-conversion retention and direct audience relationships.

### PHASE 3: OPERATIONAL CHRONOGRAM & DEPLOYMENT
Generate a tactical content chronogram skeleton utilizing a Markdown table:
1. Format template precisely:
| Day | Channel | Medium | Conceptual Pillar | Core Message Focus | Hook/Angle Type |
2. Include specific directives on "what to post" to ensure the production assets perfectly match the strategic intent.

## Constraints
- Do not use generic cross-posting templates. Treat every medium natively.
- Never use empty corporate buzzwords; maintain concrete, data-backed analytical rigor.
