## Phase 1: Brand Guidelines & Positioning Territory

### 1. Core Value Proposition
Maestro is a lightweight, zero-dashboard, real-time contextual bridge (powered by passive DOM reading) that unifies sales threads across Gmail, LinkedIn, WhatsApp Web, and CRM systems directly inside the browser. It eliminates admin cognitive overhead and manual data logging, allowing sales reps to focus entirely on selling without entering data or switching tabs.

### 2. Verbal & Visual Tone Constraints
*   **Verbal Tone Constraints:**
    *   **High-Fidelity & Precise:** Never use vague corporate buzzwords (e.g., "AI revolution," "leverage synergy," "paradigm shift"). Use concrete, data-backed operational terms (e.g., "DOM-scraping response latency," "lead leakage," "CRM-to-browser context matching").
    *   **Direct & Rep-Centric:** Speak to the pain of the SDR/BDR. Avoid fluffy academic sentences. Write like an experienced Sales Operations Director who understands the fatigue of manual data entry.
    *   **Regional Relevance:** Incorporate standard terminology from the Brazilian inside sales ecosystem (e.g., "vendas complexas," "taxa de abertura," "leads frios," "funil de vendas").
*   **Visual Tone Constraints:**
    *   **Zero-Dashboard Minimalism:** Never show mockups of complex dashboards or charts. The product is invisible by design. Focus visual assets exclusively on browser overlays (e.g., green indicators on WhatsApp Web, orange side-panels on LinkedIn).
    *   **High Contrast UI Interactions:** Show raw UI interaction state. Highlight the extension matching names, email addresses, and phone numbers in the active tab (DOM) with records from the CRM.
    *   **Reality-Focused Imagery:** No generic corporate stock photography. Use clean dark/light mode screenshots of the browser showing Chrome's extension permissions and local data flows.

### 3. Strategic Territories
We define exactly three distinct conceptual territories that Maestro can authentically claim:

#### Territory 1: The "Anti-Prompting, Zero-Dashboard" Workflow
*   **Concept:** Current AI sidebars (Gemini for Workspace, Claude Cowork) require the sales representative to act as a "prompt engineer"—copying customer text, pasting it into a chat box, formulating instructions, and then copying the result back. Maestro sits passively in the browser's DOM, reads context in real-time, and automatically prompts itself to suggest next steps without a dashboard or text input field.
*   **Target Audience:** High-turnover SDR/BDR teams and PJ/MEI freelancers tired of administrative bloat.
*   **Key Message:** *Stop prompting. Start selling. The first AI that reads your browser context and acts, zero typing required.*

#### Territory 2: The "Privacy-by-Default" Procurement Shortcut
*   **Concept:** Enterprise sales teams want to adopt AI tools, but their compliance, legal, and security departments block them due to concerns over customer data leaks to foreign LLM training sets. Maestro operates under strict local-only execution policies, processing DOM updates locally and ensuring that sensitive customer CRM data never leaves the local browser session to train public models.
*   **Target Audience:** Sales Operations (SalesOps), IT Managers, and Security Officers in mid-market B2B companies.
*   **Key Message:** *The AI sales assistant approved by security on Day 1. Local-only DOM reading means zero data leaves your browser to train public models.*

#### Territory 3: The WhatsApp-First Sales Hub (The Brazilian Inside Sales Reality)
*   **Concept:** B2B sales in Brazil do not run on email; they run on WhatsApp (which boasts a 98% open rate). However, standard sales tools are built for the US market, completely ignoring WhatsApp and resulting in massive "lead leakage" as reps fail to log WhatsApp chats to CRM. Maestro is designed specifically for the Brazilian reality, acting as the bridge that anchors WhatsApp Web directly to Salesforce, HubSpot, or RD Station.
*   **Target Audience:** Inside sales operations in the Southeast and South regions of Brazil (São Paulo, Santa Catarina, Rio Grande do Sul).
*   **Key Message:** *Your CRM is blind to WhatsApp. Maestro brings your CRM, LinkedIn, and Gmail threads into WhatsApp Web passively, stopping lead abandonment in its tracks.*

## Phase 2: Content Direction & Medium Selection

For each strategic territory, the distribution architecture is mapped strictly based on distribution mechanics and audience consumption friction:

### Territory 1: The "Anti-Prompting, Zero-Dashboard" Workflow
*   **Video (Short-form):**
    *   *Format:* High-speed screen captures (under 30 seconds) showing a split-screen.
    *   *Left:* "The Standard Way" (rep opening a Claude sidebar, copying an email, drafting a prompt, fixing it, pasting it back - 45 seconds).
    *   *Right:* "The Maestro Way" (rep opens WhatsApp Web, Maestro automatically reads the DOM and suggests a contextual reply - 3 seconds).

    *   *Format:* Short, punchy LinkedIn posts targeted at Sales Leaders questioning why their reps spend 60% of their day on administrative work. Focus on the metric of "Cognitive Context-Switching Tax" (which reduces productive selling time by 40%).

    *   *Format:* Mid-funnel drip emails containing tactical breakdowns of how removing 3 clicks per email template translates to an extra 40 cold touches per day per SDR.

### Territory 2: The "Privacy-by-Default" Procurement Shortcut
*   **Written (Social):**
    *   *Format:* Text-only LinkedIn articles and posts targeting CTOs and SalesOps directors. Deconstruct the difference between API-driven security and local extension-based security. Emphasize why Chrome's local-only execution policy is the fastest procurement bypass.
*   **Email:**
    *   *Format:* Outbound campaigns to B2B IT leaders offering a template "IT Clearance Security Form for Browser Extensions."
*   **Video (Long-form):**
    *   *Format:* A 3-minute technical architecture walk-through showing developer console traffic logs to prove that no customer CRM data is transmitted to external server logs during local DOM execution.

### Territory 3: The WhatsApp-First Sales Hub (The Brazilian Inside Sales Reality)
*   **Video (Short-form):**
    *   *Format:* Highly engaging, high-contrast Reels/TikToks/LinkedIn videos demonstrating "Lead Leakage." The video starts with a mobile notification of an unanswered WhatsApp message from a hot client, showing how the rep forgot to log it to HubSpot, resulting in an abandoned lead.
*   **Written (Social):**
    *   *Format:* Thought leadership posts in prominent Brazilian sales communities (Meetime, RD Station, sales slack groups). Share local data (e.g., "Why Southeast sales teams lose 32% of prospects because WhatsApp isn't integrated with CRM").
*   **Email:**
    *   *Format:* Weekly newsletters sharing regional sales benchmarks, contrasting Brazilian WhatsApp-centric sales habits with US email-centric SaaS designs.
